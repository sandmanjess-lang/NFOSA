About the NFO

The National Financial Ombud Scheme (NFO) is an independent, free dispute resolution service for financial services consumers in South Africa. Established in 2022 through the amalgamation of several legacy ombud schemes, the NFO provides a single point of access for consumers with complaints about banking, credit and insurance services.

The NFO is recognised by the Financial Sector Conduct Authority (FSCA) and the Ombud Council as an approved ombud scheme under the Financial Sector Regulation Act.

Yes. The NFO's dispute resolution service is entirely free for consumers. You will not be charged for submitting a complaint, for any stage of the investigation, or for any determination issued by the NFO.

You also do not need to hire a lawyer or legal representative — the process is designed to be accessible without professional assistance.

Yes. The NFO is fully independent from the financial institutions whose complaints it handles. It is not owned by, controlled by, or affiliated with any financial institution or industry body. Its independence is critical to its ability to adjudicate complaints fairly.

Eligibility

Any financial services consumer in South Africa who has an unresolved complaint against a financial provider that is a registered NFO participant may submit a complaint. This includes individuals and small businesses.

Before approaching the NFO, you must first raise your complaint directly with the financial provider and allow them at least 20 business days to respond.

The NFO handles complaints across three divisions:

  • Banking & Credit: Bank accounts, home loans, personal loans, credit cards, vehicle finance, credit bureaus, debit orders
  • Non-Life Insurance: Motor, home, travel, commercial and other short-term insurance
  • Life Insurance: Life cover, funeral policies, disability cover, retirement annuities, health insurance

There are certain complaints that fall outside the NFO's jurisdiction, including:

  • Complaints about providers who are not registered NFO participants
  • Complaints that are subject to ongoing legal proceedings
  • Complaints that have already been determined by another ombud or court
  • Complaints submitted outside prescribed time limits

If your complaint falls outside our jurisdiction, we will advise you on other available avenues. View other ombuds and trade bodies.

Submitting a Complaint

You can submit a complaint in several ways:

  • Online: Use the complaint form on this website
  • Email: info@nfosa.co.za
  • Phone: 0860 800 900 (a consultant will assist you)
  • Post: Write to us at our postal address (see Contact Us)

All methods are free. Include as much supporting documentation as possible.

No. You do not need a lawyer or any legal representative to submit a complaint to the NFO. The process is designed to be accessible to all consumers. Our staff can assist you through the process if needed — simply call us on 0860 800 900.

Include all relevant supporting documents, such as:

  • Correspondence with the financial provider (emails, letters, SMS)
  • Account statements or policy documents
  • Contracts or agreements
  • Photos or other evidence relevant to your complaint
  • Any reference numbers provided by the provider

Do not submit original documents — copies are sufficient.

The Process

Timeframes vary depending on the complexity of the complaint. The NFO will acknowledge your complaint within 5 business days. An initial assessment is completed within 10–20 business days. The full investigation and determination period varies based on the nature of the complaint and the cooperation of all parties.

You can track the status of your complaint at any time using your case reference number.

You can track the status of your complaint using the Track Your Complaint tool on this website. You will need your case reference number, which was provided in your acknowledgement correspondence.

If you cannot find your reference number, contact us on 0860 800 900 or at info@nfosa.co.za.

Outcomes

If the NFO's determination is in your favour, the participant (financial provider) is legally bound to comply with the determination within the specified timeframe. The NFO monitors compliance. If the participant fails to comply, contact the NFO immediately.

If you are unhappy with the NFO's determination, you retain the right to pursue other legal remedies available to you, including approaching the courts. Using the NFO process does not prevent you from taking legal action.

However, note that once a determination has been accepted, it is binding on both parties.

Participants

You can submit a complaint about financial providers that are registered participants of the NFO. Most major South African banks, credit providers and insurers are registered participants. View the full list of participants to check whether your provider is covered.

Financial institutions can apply to become NFO participants. Participation may be mandatory for certain categories of regulated financial service providers under the Financial Sector Regulation Act. Find out more about becoming a participant.
