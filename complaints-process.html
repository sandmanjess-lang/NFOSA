How it works

The Complaints Process

A step-by-step guide to submitting and resolving your financial complaint through the NFO — free, fair and independent.

Quick links: Step-by-step process Process flowchart Timeframes Application for assistance Submit a Complaint →

Important – before you approach the NFO: You must first raise your complaint directly with your financial services provider. Give them at least 20 business days to resolve the matter. Only contact the NFO if your complaint remains unresolved or you are unhappy with their response.

Step by Step

How the complaints process works

The NFO process is designed to be straightforward and accessible for all consumers.

1

Complain to your financial provider first

You must first submit your complaint directly to your bank, insurer or credit provider. Request that your complaint be directed to their complaints department or internal ombud.

Keep a record of all correspondence — dates, reference numbers and the names of people you spoke to. This information will be useful when you approach the NFO.

Allow at least 20 business days for the provider to respond before escalating to the NFO.
2

Submit your complaint to the NFO

If your complaint remains unresolved, submit it to the NFO. You can do this:

  • Online: Use our complaint form on this website
  • Email: info@nfosa.co.za
  • Phone: 0860 800 900 (a consultant will help you)
  • Post: Write to us at our postal address

Include all relevant documents: your correspondence with the provider, account statements, policy documents, and any other supporting evidence.

Submit Online Now
3

Acknowledgement and initial assessment

We will acknowledge receipt of your complaint and conduct an initial assessment to determine whether it falls within our jurisdiction.

We may contact you for additional information or documents during this stage. If your complaint is outside our jurisdiction, we will advise you on other avenues available to you.

4

Investigation

The NFO requests a full response from the financial provider and reviews all evidence submitted by both parties. Our investigators may seek additional information from either party.

The investigation process is impartial — our role is to determine the facts and apply the applicable rules and legislation fairly.

5

Recommendation or Determination

Based on the investigation, the NFO may issue a recommendation for resolution — giving both parties an opportunity to agree to a settlement. If agreement is not reached, a formal determination will be issued.

Determinations are binding on participants (the financial providers). Consumers retain the right to pursue other legal remedies if they are unhappy with the determination.

6

Resolution and closure

If the determination is in your favour, the participant must comply with the NFO's decision within the specified timeframe. The NFO monitors compliance and can take action against participants who fail to comply.

If you accept the determination, your complaint is closed. If you are unhappy with the outcome, you may pursue other legal avenues — but note that legal proceedings will not be stayed pending an appeal.

What to expect

Timeframes

Understanding how long each stage of the process takes helps set realistic expectations.

Acknowledgement

5 days

We will acknowledge your complaint within 5 business days of receipt.

Initial Assessment

10–20 days

An initial assessment of jurisdiction and eligibility is completed within 10–20 business days.

Full Investigation

Varies

Timeframes vary depending on complexity. The NFO aims to resolve complaints as efficiently as possible.

You can track the status of your complaint at any time using your case reference number.

Need Help?

Application for Assistance

If you need help completing your complaint form or understanding the process, our team is available to assist you. We can guide you through each step and ensure your complaint is submitted correctly.

This service is particularly available for vulnerable consumers, including those with limited digital literacy or access, elderly consumers, or those with disabilities.
