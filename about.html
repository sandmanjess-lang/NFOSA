Who we are

About the NFO

The National Financial Ombud Scheme (NFO) is South Africa's independent body for resolving disputes between financial services consumers and providers — fairly and free of charge.

Mission, Vision & Values

Our purpose and principles

Our Mission

To provide accessible, efficient, fair and independent resolution of disputes between financial services consumers and providers in South Africa.

Our Vision

To be a trusted, respected and effective financial ombud that strengthens consumer confidence in South Africa's financial services sector.

Our Structure

Our Divisions

The NFO operates three specialist divisions covering the full spectrum of regulated financial services in South Africa.

Banking & Credit Division

Handles complaints about banking services and credit providers, including the formerly separate Banking and Credit Divisions. This includes bank accounts, home loans, personal loans, credit cards, vehicle finance and credit bureaus.

Covers: Banks, credit providers, credit bureaus

Banking Participants Credit Participants

Non-Life Insurance Division

Handles complaints about short-term insurance products. This includes motor vehicle insurance, home and household insurance, travel insurance, commercial insurance and other non-life insurance products.

Covers: Short-term insurers and intermediaries

Life Insurance Division

Handles complaints about long-term insurance products. This includes life insurance, funeral cover, disability cover, retirement annuities, health insurance and other long-term insurance products.

Our Independence

Why our independence matters

The NFO is a fully independent body. We are not owned by, controlled by, or affiliated with any financial institution, industry body or government department.

Our independence is critical to our ability to adjudicate complaints fairly and impartially. Consumers and participants alike can be confident that our decisions are based solely on the facts of each case and the applicable rules.

The NFO is recognised by the Financial Sector Conduct Authority (FSCA) and the Ombud Council as an approved ombud scheme under the Financial Sector Regulation Act.

What our independence means for you

  • ✓ Decisions based purely on the merits of each case
  • ✓ No conflicts of interest with financial providers
  • ✓ Determinations binding on participants
  • ✓ Regulated and recognised by the FSCA and Ombud Council

Our Rules

The NFO operates under a formal set of Rules that govern how complaints are received, investigated and determined.

What the Rules cover

  • Who may submit a complaint to the NFO
  • What types of complaints are within jurisdiction
  • Timeframes for submitting complaints
  • How complaints are investigated
  • How determinations are issued and enforced
  • Rights and obligations of all parties

Accessing the Rules

The NFO's Rules are publicly available. Consumers and participants are encouraged to familiarise themselves with the Rules before submitting or responding to a complaint.

Accessibility & Inclusion

Support for Vulnerable Consumers

The NFO is committed to making its services accessible to all South Africans, including those facing additional challenges.

We recognise that some consumers may face additional barriers when engaging with financial services or with the NFO's process. These may include consumers who:

  • Have limited digital access or literacy
  • Are elderly or have disabilities
  • Face language barriers
  • Are experiencing financial distress
  • Are in remote or rural areas

The NFO has specific measures in place to support these consumers through the complaints process.

Phone Support

Our consultants can assist you to submit a complaint by phone at no cost.

Community

Corporate Social Responsibility

The NFO is committed to contributing positively to financial inclusion and consumer education in South Africa.

Through outreach programmes, financial literacy initiatives and community partnerships, the NFO works to ensure that all South Africans — particularly underserved communities — are aware of their rights as financial services consumers and have access to fair dispute resolution.
