Other Ombuds & Trade Bodies

If your complaint falls outside the NFO's jurisdiction, one of the following bodies may be able to assist you.

The NFO is not responsible for the content or services of external organisations listed below. Contact these bodies directly for information on their jurisdiction and processes.

Financial Sector Conduct Authority (FSCA)

The FSCA regulates and supervises the conduct of financial institutions in South Africa. If your complaint is about the conduct of an FSP, the FSCA may be able to assist.

Website: www.fsca.co.za

Ombud Council

The Ombud Council oversees the functioning of all recognised financial sector ombuds in South Africa, including the NFO.

Website: www.ombudcouncil.org.za

National Credit Regulator (NCR)

The NCR regulates the South African credit industry. For complaints about registered credit providers not covered by the NFO, the NCR may be able to assist.

Website: www.ncr.org.za

South African Reserve Bank (SARB)

The Prudential Authority within the SARB regulates and supervises banks and insurers for safety and soundness. Consumer complaints are generally handled by the NFO.

Website: www.resbank.co.za

National Consumer Commission (NCC)

The NCC handles complaints under the Consumer Protection Act, which may apply to certain financial services transactions.

Website: www.thencc.gov.za

Pension Funds Adjudicator

The Pension Funds Adjudicator handles complaints about pension funds registered under the Pension Funds Act. Retirement annuity complaints may fall under either this office or the NFO depending on the product.

Website: www.pfa.org.za

Not sure which body handles your complaint?

Contact us and we'll help point you in the right direction — even if your complaint is outside the NFO's jurisdiction.
